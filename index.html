Detailed Projects

Baja Central Reciever Node

Designed a PCB hat to connect to a Raspberry Pi Zero 2W to collect data over a CAN network and wirelessly transmit it back to a pit station. Utilizes an SX1262 LoRa chip for wireless transmission up to ~2 miles. The central receiver also features a MAX-M10 GPS Module to collect GPS data, including heading, real speed, altitude, and location.

PCB Reflow Oven

Created a PCB reflow oven from scratch to solder small SMD components for Purdue Baja Racing. Used sheet metal, fiberglass insulation, JB weld, and electronic components to control and display the heating curve inside the oven. Reliably able to reflow solder paste to the boards.

Table Tennis Robot - Purdue SPARK Challenge

Worked alongside a team of three other Purdue Engineering students to design, manufacture, assemble, and run an automatic table tennis robot. The robot clamped onto the table and used aluminum rails and brushless motors to move a ping pong paddle along the X, Y, and Z axes. The robot used a color-detecting camera to see a bright pink ping pong ball and move along the axes, and return the ball to the human player.
